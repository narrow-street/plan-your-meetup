When Do Meetups Happen?¶

By Ruosi Wang 2019/2/7

Overview:¶

Every week, there are hundreds and thousands of meetup events going on in each city. When do these meetup events often happen? What are the occurrence patterns during weekdays and weekends? Are there any differences among different meetup topics?

To answer these questions, we first used a case example to demonstrate the possible differences among different topics by comparing social and career-business events in Boston. Then, meetup events in all the cities were aggregated to reveal the daily occurrence patterns and hourly occurrence patterns.

Knowing these insights will help meetup hosts schedule their events at the most appealing time.

A Case Comparision: Social vs. Career-business Events in Boston¶

import viz_explorative as vz
vz.plot_compare_occurrence('Boston', ['Social', 'career-business'])
Loading BokehJS ...

In the above plots, we compared two topics of meetup events (Social vs. Career-business) on their occurrence patterns. Each circle represents a meetup event. The y-axis shows the day of a week, and the x-axis shows the start time. For clarity of viewing, we only displayed the events in Boston between 2015 to 2017.

This case of comparison revealed some similarities and distinctions between the two examined topics. Let's first start with the similarity. Both types of events had some peak time, usually around dinner time and lunch time. Though, the range of peak time was more extensive for social than career-business events. One of the main differences is the weekday/weekend difference. Career-business events happened less often during weekdays than weekdays. In contrast, weekends are a very busy time for social events.

In the following parts, we addressed the daily and hourly occurrence patterns separately with the whole dataset.

Daily Occurrance¶

In [2]:
vz.plot_daily_occurrence('US', topic_N=15)

The above plot showed the daily occurrence rates of different event topics. For example, the cell at the top-left corner represents the percentage (10.6%) of career-business events that took places on Mondays (averaged across all the cities between 2011 and 2018). The colors of the cells indicated the levels of occurrence rates. The darker the color, the more frequent the occurrence.

To better illustrate the differences, we sorted the topics by their event occurrence rates during the weekends. As the plot depicted, career-business and tech meetup events happened mostly during weekdays between Monday and Thursday (around 80%). In comparison, other topics of events occurred more often on the weekends, with outdoors-adventure events having the highest rates. Besides, there was a general trend of events being held more frequently on Saturday than Sunday, except for the topic of beliefs.

Hourly Occurrance¶

In [3]:
vz.plot_hourly_occurrence('US', ['social', 'sports-fitness', 'career-business', 'beliefs'])

Here, we showed the kernel density(KDE) plots of the hourly occurrence rates on the top four topics with the most number of events to achieve better clarity.

During weekdays, all the topis showed similar patterns, in which the peaks were mostly around 6 pm but with some variations in widths and heights. During the weekends, in contrast, the distributions of events spread more widely and varied more drastically. Sports-fitness events had the earliest peak at around 9 am, and coming in next were beliefs events. Both career-business and social events reached a climax at noonish. In the evening, there were another set of peaks at around 6 pm.

Summary¶

Everybody knows that timing is critical, and this is also true for hosting meetup events. The above analyses indicated that meetup members did have preferences in scheduling meetup events.

  • They tent to host and participate in professional development events more often on weekdays and join leisure events more often on weekends.
  • Early evening was the most frequent option for weekdays. However, people have far more choices during weekends.






Methods¶

The dataset was fetched from the Meetup API utilizing a python API client. In particular, We first identified the Meetup groups(>100 members) in the top 30 US cities with the most number of groups and then gathered the meetup details associated with these groups between 2011 and 2018 July.

We implemented the above explorative analysis with PySpark, please see here for the source code. The datetime data was converted to the local time of the respective meetup location.
